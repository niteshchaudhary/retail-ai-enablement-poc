H-E-B Retail Knowledge Base

COMPANY OVERVIEW
H-E-B is one of the largest independently owned food retailers in the United States.
H-E-B operates over 420 stores throughout Texas and Mexico.
H-E-B generates annual sales of over $48 billion.
H-E-B employs over 145,000 partners (employees).
H-E-B is headquartered in San Antonio, Texas.

PRODUCT CATALOG MANAGEMENT
H-E-B manages over 50,000 SKUs across fresh produce, packaged goods, and private label products.
Products are categorized by department, aisle, and shelf placement.
H-E-B private label brands include H-E-B, Central Market, and Hill Country Fare.
New products go through a vendor onboarding process including quality checks and compliance review.
Product data is maintained in a master product catalog synced across all stores and digital channels.

PRICING STRATEGY
Pricing is updated daily based on competitor analysis and demand forecasting models.
H-E-B uses dynamic pricing for perishable goods to reduce waste.
Promotional pricing is planned weeks in advance and coordinated with vendor partners.
Price optimization models consider elasticity, competitor prices, and inventory levels.
H-E-B price matches competitors in local markets to remain competitive.

DEMAND FORECASTING
H-E-B uses ML models to forecast demand at store and SKU level.
Forecasting models are retrained weekly using point-of-sale data.
Accurate forecasting reduces food waste and improves on-shelf availability.
Demand forecasts drive replenishment orders, staffing, and promotional planning.
External factors like weather, holidays, and local events are incorporated into forecast models.
Forecast accuracy is measured using MAPE (Mean Absolute Percentage Error).

INVENTORY MANAGEMENT
Inventory levels are tracked in real time across all stores and distribution centers.
Automated replenishment triggers are based on reorder point models.
Out-of-stock events are flagged and escalated to store managers automatically.
H-E-B operates multiple distribution centers across Texas to support store replenishment.
Shrink (inventory loss due to theft, damage, or expiry) is tracked and reported daily.
Safety stock levels are calculated based on demand variability and lead times.

SUPPLY CHAIN
H-E-B works with thousands of local and national vendors.
H-E-B prioritizes local Texas vendors as part of its community commitment.
Vendor scorecards track on-time delivery, fill rate, and quality compliance.
Supply chain disruptions are monitored in real time and alternative sourcing is activated when needed.
H-E-B owns and operates its own trucking fleet for distribution.
Cold chain management is critical for fresh produce and dairy products.
When supply chain disruptions occur, H-E-B activates alternative sourcing strategies to ensure store replenishment continues without interruption.

CUSTOMER PERSONALIZATION
H-E-B uses purchase history and browsing behavior to personalize promotions.
Personalization models are updated nightly and served via the H-E-B app and website.
Customer segments are defined by purchase frequency, basket size, and brand affinity.
Personalized digital coupons are delivered through the H-E-B app.
Customer lifetime value models help prioritize marketing spend across segments.
H-E-B loyalty data is used to improve product recommendations and store layout decisions.

STORE OPERATIONS
Each H-E-B store is managed by a store director supported by department managers.
Store performance is measured by sales per square foot, shrink rate, and customer satisfaction scores.
Store associates are trained on customer service, food safety, and inventory handling.
H-E-B uses workforce management tools to optimize staffing based on foot traffic forecasts.
Store layouts are optimized using customer traffic pattern analysis.

AI ENABLEMENT AT H-E-B
MLOps pipelines manage the lifecycle of demand forecasting and personalization models.
LLMOps frameworks support internal chatbots and customer facing AI assistants.
RAG systems are used to ground AI responses in H-E-B product and policy data.
LangSmith is used for LLM observability and tracing across all AI pipelines.
AI governance policies ensure models are auditable, explainable, and free from bias.
Feature stores centralize reusable ML features across demand forecasting and personalization models.
Model performance is monitored continuously and retraining is triggered when drift is detected.
